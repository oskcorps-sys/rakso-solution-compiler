# RAKSO Solution Compiler Doctrine

## Mission

Convert business chaos into sellable, executable solutions.

## Core formula

```txt
Reality -> Evidence -> Diagnosis -> Money -> Offer -> Contract -> Execution
```

## Non-negotiable principles

### 1. Evidence before architecture

No solution is designed before evidence is captured and classified.

Evidence levels:

- `client_stated`
- `transcript_confirmed`
- `document_confirmed`
- `public_verified`
- `sector_inferred`
- `assumption`
- `unknown`

### 2. Pain must translate to money

A pain that cannot be connected to time loss, revenue leakage, risk reduction, capacity increase, or operational cost is not ready for a paid solution.

### 3. The simplest solution that captures value wins

Do not build an agentic workflow when a checklist, form, spreadsheet, or reminder workflow solves the business problem.

### 4. Offer before implementation

Every solution must be explainable, sellable, scoped, and economically justified before being converted into an SDD+ contract.

### 5. SDD+ is the contract, not the imagination engine

SDD+ formalizes a justified decision. It does not invent the business case.

### 6. Execution is adapter-based

The compiler must not be locked to Claude, Codex, Antigravity, OpenAI, n8n, Make, Zapier, Hermes, or any single runtime.

### 7. Critical Lens is transversal

Every major artifact is audited for assumptions, overengineering, vendor lock-in, scope creep, missing evidence, operational fragility, and commercial weakness.

## What this system must not become

- A generic prompt library
- A hype-driven AI automation generator
- A Claude-only skill system
- A Codex-only implementation repo
- A lead research toy
- A proposal generator detached from evidence
- A custom app factory that defaults to overbuilding

## What this system must become

An internal operating system for transforming business reality into:

1. evidence ledger
2. operational diagnosis
3. pain-to-profit map
4. offer brief
5. SDD+ contract
6. executable artifacts
7. deployment package
8. market expansion loop
